COURSE OUTCOME

By the end of Credibility Dynamics, participants will be able to:

1. Identify when credibility is being influenced by non-evidence factors such as authority, emotion, role, or social dynamics

2. Distinguish clearly between what is observed and what is inferred, reducing errors caused by assumption

3. Generate and maintain multiple plausible explanations for behavior or responses without collapsing into premature conclusions

4. Recognize the purpose of an interaction (evaluation, support, decision-making, or relationship maintenance) and adjust their approach accordingly

5. Use neutral, non-confrontational communication strategies to clarify reasoning, reintroduce evidence, or maintain alignment

6. Maintain cognitive flexibility under stress, avoiding rigid or reactive interpretation

7. Accumulate patterns over time rather than relying on single interactions when forming judgments

8. Make decisions based on evidence and context, even when information is incomplete or ambiguous

Participants will leave with a disciplined and adaptable way of thinking that improves how they interpret situations, communicate with others, and make decisions under uncertainty.
